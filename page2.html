The Pawn
Each player begins the game with eight pawns placed along their second rank. A pawn may move by vertically advancing to a vacant square ahead. The first time a pawn moves, it has the additional option of vertically advancing two squares, provided that both squares are vacant. Unlike other pieces, the pawn can only move forwards. Pawns can capture pieces one square diagonally ahead of them. Pawn structures show the different things possible in a position. Pawns are very important in openings to determine pawn structures. The rule of en-passant allows a pawn to capture an opposing pawn that has just moved two squares forward from its starting position, as if it had only moved one square forward.
